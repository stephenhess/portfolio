Finding a great health plan

Health insurance is tough. Stride Health crunches the numbers for consumers and recommends plans based on their quality and affordability. I led design through the 2014-2015 Open Enrollment period. We designed and launched a complete rebuild of the product experience in four months, transitioning it from a single page desktop app to a fully responsive, mobile first experience.

One step at a time

The Stride Health app simplifies the process of finding health insurance by breaking down what is typically a complex decision into a number of simple questions that lead you to the right conclusion. "Don't Make Me Think" and avoiding "The Tyranny of Choice" were big inspirations for this approach.

Designing for many devices

In our redesign of Stride Health, the site transformed from a desktop only experience to something that could be accessed on any phone, tablet or PC. I used Edge Reflow to design the grid system and how major structural and navigational elements adapt across a range of viewports.

Partnering with the on-demand economy

I designed and spec-ed integrations with the best companies in the on-demand economy, including Uber, Postmates and TaskRabbit, with the goal of helping their 1099 workers find health insurance.

Comparing plans

At the end of the day, the quality and cost of care is what matters - not the deductible, premium, max out-of-pocket, co-insurance, network size. Plus who even knows what all of these mean? By pulling out the most important summary stats, using color indicators and providing a way to vet plans in real life scenarios, the Stride Health products helps folks make a great decision with little effort.
